# Watermarking Methods Table

Updated: 2026-06-28

Scope: this table covers the watermarking methods currently registered under `evaluator/watermarking/methods/`. Image-size and capacity notes describe the current local wrappers, not only the upstream papers. For variable-size methods, the listed capacity is the wrapper default or the packaged checkpoint capacity.

## Summary

- Capacity tiers: `chunkyseal` is the current high-capacity outlier at 1024 bits; `videoseal`, `pixelseal`, and `mbrs` sit at 256 bits; `trustmark*`, `invismark`, `vine`, and `stegastamp` are 100-bit methods; `wam`, `maskwm-d32`, `hidden`, `cin`, `pimog`, and `ssl-watermarking` are 30 to 32-bit methods; `rawatermark` is zero-bit detection.
- Size behavior matters for fair benchmarks: `cin`, `pimog`, `hidden`, `mbrs`, `maskwm-d32`, `rawatermark`, and `stegastamp` produce fixed-size outputs, while the invisible-watermark variants, TrustMark, VideoSeal-family methods, WAM, SSL watermarking, and InvisMark keep or restore the original image size in the local wrappers.
- The remaining no-weight classical baselines are the two ShieldMnt invisible-watermark variants, useful for speed and interpretability but usually weaker against resizing, crop, and recompression than modern neural methods.
- Neural methods cover different use cases: TrustMark is compact and arbitrary-resolution; VideoSeal/PixelSeal favor robust image/video messages; ChunkySeal tests high payload capacity; WAM adds localization; RAWatermark is a fast detector-only watermark; StegaStamp targets physical print-camera recovery.
- Local packaging exceptions are intentional: `pixelseal`, `chunkyseal`, and `invismark` have large checkpoints, and `chunkyseal` is above the strict subsecond embed gate locally, but they are included because they are required benchmark algorithms.
- `vine` is a diffusion-dependent registration exception requested for this workspace: local VINE-R and SD-Turbo weights are packaged under `resources/weights/watermarking/vine/`, full embed/extract smoke passes, and warm-loaded CUDA timing is subsecond for both embed and extract on the local RTX 5070 Laptop GPU.
